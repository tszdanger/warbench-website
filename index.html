SECTION 03

Key Findings

FINDING 1

Structural Brittleness

Current LLMs exhibit extreme structural brittleness in tactical reasoning. While demonstrating functional baseline competence in conventional, symmetric environments, model decision quality systematically collapses when subjected to complex terrain (urban/mountainous) or high force asymmetry.

FINDING 2

Legal Compliance Failure

Current LLMs fail to reliably respect IHL in tactical scenarios. Superficial alignment guardrails (i.e., refusal rates) are completely decoupled from actual operational compliance. Violations stem primarily from structural inability to identify contextual constraints.

FINDING 3

Edge Deployment Non-Viable

Current edge-optimized models are practically non-viable for real-time tactical deployment. Aggressive weight quantization fails the 5-second threshold while triggering catastrophic collapse in decision quality and legal compliance. Ethical guardrails degrade faster than reasoning.

FINDING 4

Fog of War Vulnerability

LLMs suffer severe performance degradation under both missing and contradictory intelligence. Rather than adapting with operational caution, models respond to contextual deficits through dangerous heuristic simplification.

FINDING 5

CoT as Safety Safeguard

Explicit reasoning steps consistently improve both tactical decision quality (+0.02) and ethical alignment (+3.8% compliance). By forcing models to state constraints prior to decision-making, CoT serves as an effective structural safeguard.

SECTION 06

Research Paper

WARBENCH: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Evaluating LLMs in Military Decision-Making

Keywords: LLM Evaluation, Military AI, Ethical AI

Evaluation Period: January - February 2026

Dataset: 136 multi-source verified historical scenarios

Abstract

Large Language Models are increasingly being considered for deployment in safety-critical military applications. However, current benchmarks suffer from structural blindspots that systematically overestimate model capabilities in real-world tactical scenarios. Existing frameworks typically ignore strict legal constraints based on International Humanitarian Law (IHL), omit edge computing limitations, lack robustness testing for fog of war, and inadequately evaluate explicit reasoning. To address these vulnerabilities, we present WARBENCH, a comprehensive evaluation framework establishing a foundational tactical baseline alongside four distinct stress testing dimensions. Through a large scale empirical evaluation of nine leading models on 136 high-fidelity historical scenarios, we reveal severe structural flaws including baseline tactical reasoning collapse, extreme operational risks in edge-optimized models (legal violation rates approaching 70%), and catastrophic performance degradation under quantization and information loss.
